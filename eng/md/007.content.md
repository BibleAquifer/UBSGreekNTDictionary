## ἡγεμονεύω (id: 002242000000000)

ἡγεμονεύω
=========

ἡγεμονεύω (G2230)

ἄγω

37\.60 · Control, Rule · Rule, Govern

to be a governor (derivative of ἡγεμών\[a] ‘ruler,’ [37\.59](#37.59)) to be a governor, in the NT restricted to the meaning of a governor of a Roman province The expression ‘Quirinius was governor of Syria’ ([LUK 2:2](https://ref.ly/Mark2:2)) may be rendered in some languages as ‘Quirinius was the one who commanded all those in the province of Syria’ or ‘Quirinius gave orders to all those in Syria.’

[LUK 2:2](https://ref.ly/Mark2:2) · [LUK 3:1](https://ref.ly/Mark3:1)

* **Associated Passages:** Luke 2:2; Luke 3:1


## ἡγεμονία (id: 002243000000000)

ἡγεμονία
========

ἡγεμονία (G2231)

noun · ἄγω

37\.58 · Control, Rule · Rule, Govern

government, rule ruling over, with the implication of providing direction and leadership In a number of languages it may be necessary to express the concept of ‘ruling’ by some form of ‘command,’ for example, ‘he commands people what they must do’ or ‘he orders people’ or ‘he is the chief over people.’

[LUK 3:1](https://ref.ly/Mark3:1)

* **Associated Passages:** Luke 3:1


## ἡγεμών (id: 002244000000000)

ἡγεμών
======

ἡγεμών (G2232)

noun · ἄγω

37\.59 · Control, Rule · Rule, Govern

ruler (derivative of ἡγέομαι\[c] ‘to rule,’ [37\.58](#37.58)) one who rules, with the implication of preeminent position In the context of [MAT 2:6](https://ref.ly/Matt2:6) it is possible to understand ‘rulers’ as a figurative reference to important places, and therefore one may also translate ‘you are by no means the least among the leading cities of Judah.’

37\.83 · Control, Rule · Rule, Govern
