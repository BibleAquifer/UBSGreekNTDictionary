## ξαίνω (id: 003480000000000)

ξαίνω
=====

ξαίνω

48\.1 · Activities Involving Cloth

to comb wool to comb wool as an initial step in the making of wool thread1 οὐ ξαίνουσιν οὐδὲ νήθουσιν οὐδὲ κοπιῶσιν ‘they do not comb wool, spin, or work hard’ [MAT 6:28](https://ref.ly/Matt6:28) (apparatus)

[MAT 6:28](https://ref.ly/Matt6:28)

1 In [MAT 6:28](https://ref.ly/Matt6:28) the Sinaiticus manuscript has an interesting reading, οὐ ξένουσιν (\= οὐ ξαίνουσιν), thus providing three processes or activities involving cloth rather than two. ## ξύλον (id: 003489000000000)

ξύλον
=====

ξύλον (G3586)

noun · ξύλινος

3\.60 · Plants · Wood and Wood Products

wood wood as a substance A number of languages make a clear distinction between wood as a substance used in artifacts and constructions and wood used for heating or cooking. A further distinction may be made between durable wood (not readily subject to rot or to the effects of termites and borers) and less durable and therefore relatively unsatisfactory wood.

[1CO 3:12](https://ref.ly/Rom3:12) · [REV 18:12](https://ref.ly/Jude18:12) · [REV 18:12](https://ref.ly/Jude18:12)

3\.61 · Plants · Wood and Wood Products

firewood wood as a substance used to burn for heat ξύλον in [LUK 23:31](https://ref.ly/Mark23:31) may also be interpreted as a live green tree (see [3\.4](#3.4)). If one assumes that the meaning here is ‘firewood,’ then it may be necessary to translate ‘wood which has not had an opportunity to dry out’ or ‘wood which is still green’ or ‘... which is still wet with sap.’

[LUK 23:31](https://ref.ly/Mark23:31)

3\.4 · Plants · Trees

It may therefore be necessary to translate ‘to eat the fruit of the tree of life’ or ‘... the fruit from the tree that causes life’ or ‘... causes people to live.’

[LUK 23:31](https://ref.ly/Mark23:31) · [GAL 3:13](https://ref.ly/2Cor3:13) · [REV 2:7](https://ref.ly/Jude2:7) · [REV 22:2](https://ref.ly/Jude22:2) · [REV 22:2](https://ref.ly/Jude22:2) · [REV 22:14](https://ref.ly/Jude22:14) · [REV 22:19](https://ref.ly/Jude22:19)
