the world below a place or region which is low As in the case of ὕψωμα\[a] ([1\.13](#1.13)), βάθος may be interpreted as a supernatural force (\[e]).  
The phrase κατώτερα μέρη τῆς γῆς ‘the lower parts of the earth’ may likewise refer to ‘the world below’ as in εἰς τὰ κατώτερα μέρη τῆς γῆς ‘down into the lower parts of the earth’ [ROM 8:39](https://ref.ly/Acts8:39) (see [83\.54](#83.54)). [ROM 8:39](https://ref.ly/Acts8:39)

28\.76 · Know · Not Able To Be Known, Secret

**Collocation:** τὰ βάθη

deep secrets, secrets difficult to find out about (figurative extension of meaning of βάθος\[a] ‘depth,’ [81\.8](#81.8)) the content of knowledge which is very difficult to know Only rarely can one use a literal rendering of τὰ βαθέα or τὰ βάθη in referring to secrets. In some instances it may be possible to use an expression such as ‘far away’ or ‘distant,’ and in other cases one can use expressions meaning ‘hard’ or ‘difficult.’ More often than not, however, the closest equivalent of τὰ βαθέα and τὰ βάθη in this type of context is either an expression of degree using a term such as ‘very’ (for example, ‘that which is very secret’) or an expression implying ‘that which is hidden’ or ‘that which is difficult to discover.’

[1CO 2:10](https://ref.ly/Rom2:10)

## βαπτίζω (id: 000906000000000)

βαπτίζω
=======

βαπτίζω (G0907)

βάπτω

53\.31 · Religious Activities · Purify, Cleanse

to wash, to purify, washing, purification to wash (in some contexts, possibly by dipping into water), with a view to making objects ritually acceptable It is also possible to understand βαπτίσωνται in [MRK 7:4](https://ref.ly/Matt7:4) as a middle form meaning ‘to wash themselves.’  
There is some doubt as to the precise extent to which βαπτίζω, καταβαπτίζω, and βαπτισμός in [MRK 7:4](https://ref.ly/Matt7:4) involve ritual cleansing, but the context would seem to imply this, particularly in view of the relationship of such washing to the rules followed by Jews in general and Pharisees in particular.

[MRK 7:4](https://ref.ly/Matt7:4) · [LUK 11:38](https://ref.ly/Mark11:38)

53\.41 · Religious Activities · Baptize

to baptize, baptism to employ water in a religious ceremony designed to symbolize purification and initiation on the basis of repentance1 According to the Didache (early second century) different forms of baptism were practiced in the early church, but with evident preference given to immersion.
