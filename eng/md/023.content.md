## ψευδόμαρτυς (id: 005461000000000)

ψευδόμαρτυς
===========

ψευδόμαρτυς (G5575a)

noun \| \-υρος \| ψευδομαρτυρέω

33\.273 · Communication · Witness, Testify

false witness (derivative of ψευδομαρτυρέω ‘to testify falsely,’ [33\.271](#33.271)) one who testifies falsely The focal element in the evidence provided by a ‘false witness’ is the fact that what is said is not true.  
It is also possible that such a person has pretended to have been present and thus an eyewitness of an event, but that is not the focus of the meaning. In fact, a false witness may have been present but at the same time may later provide false evidence as to what took place. It is possible to render ψευδόμαρτυς in some languages as ‘one who pretends to know about something but is really lying in what he says’ or ‘one who lies about what he pretends to have seen.’

[MAT 26:60](https://ref.ly/Matt26:60) · [1CO 15:15](https://ref.ly/Rom15:15)

* **Associated Passages:** Matthew 26:60; 1 Corinthians 15:15

## ψηλαφάω (id: 005468000000000)

ψηλαφάω
=======

ψηλαφάω (G5584)

aor.opt.3sg. ψηλαφήσειεν · 3pl. \-σειαν, \-σαιεν, \-σαισαν \| ψώχω

24\.76 · Sensory Events and States · Touch, Feel

to touch, to feel, to handle, to feel around for to touch by feeling and handling, implying movement over a surface There are a number of problems involved in the rendering of [1JN 1:1](https://ref.ly/2Pet1:1), for there are several radical shifts in figurative meaning. The specific reference is to ‘the Word of life’ which seems like a purposely ambiguous reference to Jesus Christ. The statement about ‘hearing the Word’ would seem to refer to a message, but the reference to ‘seeing’ implies some type of object. To emphasize the concrete nature of such an object (namely, the person of Christ), this statement about ‘touching’ is included, but in some languages a reference to ‘feeling and handling’ might imply quite a wrong connotation or even improper activity, particularly if the object involved is specifically a person.

## ψῆφος (id: 005470000000000)

ψῆφος
=====

noun \| \-ου \| ψώχω

2\.27 · Natural Substances · Earth, Mud, Sand, Rock

pebble, small stone a small stone, possibly used for casting a vote or as a magical amulet A number of different suggestions have been made as to the reference of ψῆφος in [REV 2:17](https://ref.ly/Jude2:17). ## ψυχή (id: 005475000000000)

ψυχή
====

ψυχή (G5590)

noun \| \-ῆς \| ψύχομαι

26\.4 · Psychological Faculties

inner self, mind, thoughts, feelings, heart, being the essence of life in terms of thinking, willing, and feeling It is also possible to render ψυχή in [PHP 1:27](https://ref.ly/Eph1:27) as ‘purpose’ or ‘desire,’ for ψυχή focuses upon the total psychological being involved in struggling for the faith.  
ψυχή\[a] is often glossed as ‘heart’ or ‘desire.’ Compare the following: περίλυπός ἐστιν ἡ ψυχή μου ἕως θανάτου ‘my heart is sorrowful even to the point of death’ [MAT 26:38](https://ref.ly/Matt26:38); ἵνα μὴ κάμητε ταῖς ψυχαῖς ὑμῶν ἐκλυόμενοι ‘so do not let your hearts become discouraged and give up’ [HEB 12:3](https://ref.ly/Phlm12:3) (for ψυχή in the idiom κάμνω τῇ ψυχῇ, see [25\.291](#25.291)); ἀλλ ὡς δοῦλοι Χριστοῦ ποιοῦντες τὸ θέλημα τοῦ θεοῦ ἐκ ψυχῆς ‘but with all your heart, do what God wants, as slaves of Christ’ [EPH 6:6](https://ref.ly/Gal6:6). In these passages, however, ψυχή refers really to the entire being of a person, so that [MAT 26:38](https://ref.ly/Matt26:38) may very well be rendered as ‘my sorrow is so great it almost kills me.’ [HEB 12:3](https://ref.ly/Phlm12:3) may also be rendered as ‘so do not let yourselves be discouraged and give up,’ and [EPH 6:6](https://ref.ly/Gal6:6) may be appropriately rendered as ‘but with your whole being, do what God wants, as slaves of Christ.’  
The meaning of ψυχή in the sense of the person as an individual is treated under ψυχή\[c] ([9\.20](#9.20)) as, for example, in [MAT 11:29](https://ref.ly/Matt11:29) (εὑρήσετε ἀνάπαυσιν ταῖς ψυχαῖς ὑμῶν ‘you will find rest for yourselves’).  
Even in those contexts in which ψυχή refers to existence beyond death, it may be referring figuratively to the person. For example, in [ACT 2:27](https://ref.ly/John2:27) (οὐκ ἐγκαταλείψεις τὴν ψυχήν μου εἰς ᾅδην one may translate ‘you will not abandon my soul to Hades’ or ‘you will not abandon me to the grave.’  
It is important to distinguish ψυχή\[a], signifying a psychological faculty, from ψυχή\[b], meaning ‘physical life, life principle’ ([23\.88](#23.88)). Note, for example, the occurrence of ψυχή\[b] in [HEB 4:12](https://ref.ly/Phlm4:12) (διϊκνούμενος ἄχρι μερισμοῦ ψυχῆς καὶ πνεύματος ‘it cuts all the way through to where physical life and spirit meet’). Note also the occurrence of ψυχή\[b] in [1TH 5:23](https://ref.ly/Col5:23) αὐτὸς δὲ ὁ θεὸς τῆς εἰρήνης ἁγιάσαι ὑμᾶς ὁλοτελεῖς, καὶ ὁλόκληρον ὑμῶν τὸ πνεῦμα καὶ ἡ ψυχὴ καὶ τὸ σῶμα ἀμέμπτως ἐν τῇ παρουσίᾳ τοῦ κυρίου ἡμῶν Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ τηρηθείη ‘may the God who gives us peace make you holy in every way and keep your whole being \- spirit, life, and body \- free from all fault at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ’).  
In certain contexts ψυχή would appear to be in contrast with καρδία ([26\.3](#26.3)) and διάνοια ([26\.14](#26.14)) as, for example, in [MAT 22:37](https://ref.ly/Matt22:37) ἀγαπήσεις κύριον τὸν θεόν σου ἐν ὅλω τῇ καρδίᾳ σου καὶ ἐν ὅλω τῇ ψυχῇ σου καὶ ἐν ὅλω τῇ διανοίᾳ σου ‘you shall love the Lord your God with all your heart and soul and mind’). Because of the three terms, some have insisted that there must be at least three quite different parts of human personality. Others, however, have concluded that instead of being three parts of personality, these are only three different perspectives which one may employ in thinking about or describing human personality. Still others would contend that the use of the three terms, καρδία, ψυχή and διάνοια, only emphasizes the totality of human personality, and no clear\-cut distinctions can possibly be made. Certainly the referents involve considerable overlapping. One could translate with complete justification [MAT 22:37](https://ref.ly/Matt22:37) as ‘you shall love the Lord with all your heart and life and mind.’ In fact, in many languages it is impossible to distinguish between καρδία, ψυχή, and διάνοια, and therefore it may be necessary to translate as ‘you shall love the Lord your God with all that you desire and with all that you think’ or ‘you must love the Lord your God with all your being.’
